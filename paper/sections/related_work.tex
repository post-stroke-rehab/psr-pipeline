\section{Related Work}

sEMG has long been studied as a non-invasive control channel for rehabilitation, prosthetics, and human-machine interaction, with performance shaped by both acquisition quality and downstream decoding choices~\citep{zheng2021semg}. That literature makes clear that signal conditioning is not a minor implementation detail: denoising, spectral shaping, and feature design directly affect the reliability of any intent-decoding model.

Sequence-oriented deep models remain a natural fit for windowed biosignal decoding because they can aggregate temporal evidence across successive frames. In particular, LSTM-based models are well suited to sequential dependencies in noisy measurements~\citep{hochreiter1997lstm}, while compact 1D CNNs can emphasize local temporal motifs and efficient inference. The current repository follows both lines by exposing a recurrent baseline and several CNN size variants under one interface.

Graph neural networks provide a complementary viewpoint by modeling structured relationships among windowed observations rather than treating the sequence only as a flat timeline~\citep{kipf2017gcn,hamilton2017graphsage,velickovic2018gat}. \repo\ uses that idea to construct a graph over window-level nodes and then applies configurable graph operators before graph-level pooling. Relative to these prior model families, the distinguishing contribution here is not a new graph formulation or recurrent block, but a repository-level benchmark that keeps preprocessing, tensorization, and metric reporting consistent across heterogeneous predictors.
